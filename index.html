Active Drag System

Designed a mechanical active drag system for integration into a high-power rocket, enabling precise altitude control by modulating aerodynamic drag in real time during the coast phase of flight. The system deploys a set of drag surfaces at variable angles in response to onboard altitude predictions, allowing the vehicle to bleed energy and hit a target apogee with high precision — without the use of additional propellant.

The design required careful consideration of actuator authority, deployment timing, structural loading under high dynamic pressure, and integration within tight volume constraints inside the airframe. The system represents a significant step in ANU Rocketry's capability to achieve precision altitude targeting for competition.

  • Mechanical drag modulation system for precision altitude control
  • Variable-angle drag surface deployment mechanism
  • Structural design for high dynamic pressure flight loading
  • Tight volumetric integration within rocket airframe
  • Supports competition altitude targeting requirements
Mechanical Design CAD Actuation Systems Structural Analysis Systems Integration

CFD Fin Angle Optimisation

Investigated the aerodynamic effect of varying rocket fin cant angle on shock structure and flow behaviour in the Mach 3 flight regime, using ANSYS CFD. The study characterised how changes in fin angle alter the bow shock geometry and associated wave drag, providing quantitative data to inform fin design trade-offs.

Work encompassed full-geometry setup, structured mesh generation, solver configuration for compressible supersonic flow, and systematic post-processing across multiple angle-of-attack cases — developing a reusable simulation pipeline applicable to future rocket aerodynamics work.

  • Parametric fin angle sweep at Mach 3
  • Bow shock geometry and wave drag characterisation
  • Full mesh generation and compressible solver pipeline
  • Post-processing across multiple simulation cases
  • Reusable workflow for future aerodynamics analysis
ANSYS CFD Mesh Generation Supersonic Flow Parametric Study Post-processing

Custom 3-Way Speaker System

Currently designing and building a full 3-way loudspeaker system entirely from scratch — spanning acoustic enclosure design, driver selection and characterisation, and passive crossover network engineering. The project applies the same rigorous first-principles methodology I use in aerospace: defining target frequency response, modelling enclosure acoustics, and iterating on crossover filter topology to achieve a smooth, flat handover between the woofer, midrange, and tweeter drivers.

The enclosure is being constructed to specific internal volume and bracing requirements derived from driver Thiele-Small parameters. The passive crossover is designed using LC filter networks, with component values selected to achieve the target crossover frequencies and impedance compensation across the full bandwidth.

  • Full 3-way system — woofer, midrange, and tweeter
  • Acoustic enclosure designed from Thiele-Small parameters
  • Passive LC crossover network engineering
  • Driver selection and frequency response characterisation
  • Enclosure construction — bracing, damping, and joinery
